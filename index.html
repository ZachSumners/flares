Sgr A* Flaring Analysis Reference Website

Sagittarius A* (Sgr A*) is the supermassive black hole at the centre of the Milky Way galaxy and the closest massive black hole to the Earth. Sgr A* is also a highly variable source, with observed changes in its emission spanning minutes to hours and extending across the electromagnetic spectrum. In particular, bright flares in the X-ray (often accompanied by variations at infrared and other wavelengths) punctuate Sgr A*â€™s persistent quiescent X-ray emission once per day on average. The approximately hour-long timescales for this pronounced X-ray variability hints at changes arising from very close to event horizon of the black hole. X-ray flares from Sgr A* thus offer an important and unique laboratory for studying accretion physics close to a massive black hole.
